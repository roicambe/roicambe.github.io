My Family

My family consists of four members: my father, Benjamin G. Cambe; my mother, Rosalie M. Cambe; and my older sister, Rea Yvonne M. Cambe. Growing up here in Ugong, Pasig, our family has a unique blend of cultures, with my mother being Kapampangan and my father, Ilocano.

My father, Benjamin, serves as a staff driver at the barangay, while my mother, Rosalie, is a dedicated housewife. My sister, Rea Yvonne, pursued a degree in psychology and currently works at a call center. Together, they have created a nurturing environment for my sister and me.

Reflecting on our family's background, I recall my father sharing stories about his own upbringing. His mother hailed from a wealthy family, with parents known as Don and Do√±a. However, family dynamics shifted, and my grandfather took on a different path resembling that of a gangster. Eventually, they settled in Pasig, renting a house before acquiring a property here in Ugong. Sadly, my grandparents are no longer with us.

On my mother's side, the story is one of resilience. Coming from a modest background in Pampanga, my grandfather worked as a hardworking farmer, while my grandmother cared for their seven children. Due to financial challenges, my mother was entrusted to her aunt. Despite the hardships, she managed to graduate from high school.

The paths of my parents crossed when they both worked in Taiwan. Their love story unfolded, and the rest became our family history. Their journey is a testament to the convergence of different backgrounds, creating a rich tapestry of experiences that has shaped our lives here in Ugong, Pasig.
